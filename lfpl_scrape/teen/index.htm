Teen Summer Reading - now through August 8 at all library locations!

Teen Summer Reading program (TSR) is open to 6th- through 12th-graders. Just read six books and complete one activity – like writing a book review or designing a new cover for a favorite book – between June 1 and August 8. Then return your completed TSR form* to any library location and receive a sling backpack and free passes to local attractions and U of L games.

Everyone who completes TSR will also be eligible to win one of these Grand Prizes: a tablet computer, an iPad mini, U of L football tickets, or a $100 gift card to Barnes & Noble.

*TSR forms can be picked up at any of our 18 library locations (printable version available here).

CODEvelop at the Southwest Regional Branch

CODEvelop is a summer program for high school students (age 14-18) interested in learning web development. Teens will develop a website and connect with mentors in the information technology (IT) field. Mentors will guide students through online lessons and assist in creating a project. 

Students will meet twice a week on Tuesdays and Thursdays from July 2 - July 30, 1 PM - 4 PM at the Southwest Regional Library to learn the basics of front end web development using the Treehouse on-line learning platform.

Perks of the program include free TARC passes to attend the sessions and the chance to win an iPad mini, courtesy of Net Tango!
